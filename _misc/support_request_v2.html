2019 — Kelowna (British Columbia)

Deployed a “symbiosis inversion” pilot: reconfiguring technology to serve land, water, and community with reciprocal sensors and community-led networks. Evidence and design artifacts are archived: ArDrive bundle.

2019–2024 — Resistance and Downplaying

Legacy structures resisted the pilot. Over time, clinical encounters downplayed TB risk despite symptoms compatible with TB at intervals. Requests for testing were discouraged or deferred.

2025 — Current, emergent concern

I remain untested for TB. Symptoms have slightly improved but may persist. Multiple medical centres suppressed testing attempts; hospital staff seemed concerned yet no definitive testing was authorized. This is a public-safety risk when testing is not equitably available.

Safety principle: When clinical judgment and symptoms suggest risk, testing pathways must be opened — not blocked — and results confirmed using validated methods.

Why anticorruption framing?

When diagnostics are inconsistently offered or discouraged, risk is shifted onto families and the public. Anticorruption is a public health function: it keeps feedback loops honest, ensures equitable access to testing, and restores trust through transparent oversight.

Concrete Actions for Safety (Canada)

1) Enable testing now

Use accepted tests for TB infection — tuberculin skin test (TST) and/or blood-based interferon-gamma release assays (IGRA). When access is blocked or results conflict with clinical judgement, arrange confirmation rather than dismissal.

2) Independent oversight

Invite external epidemiology and governance review to assess whether policies or practices are suppressing appropriate testing or follow-up.

3) Protect children and vulnerable groups

Prioritize outreach and low-barrier testing where incidence and consequences are higher (e.g., some Indigenous communities and newcomers), consistent with federal surveillance and standards.

4) Documentation

Maintain dated notes, encounter IDs, and correspondence. Preserve in secure archives (e.g., ArDrive) and provide to oversight bodies as requested.

Why vigilance matters

Canada’s overall incidence is low, but national surveillance shows thousands of cases yearly, with higher burden in specific populations. Testing access and timely confirmation reduce risk to families and communities.

Pourquoi la vigilance compte

Le Canada présente une faible incidence globale, mais la surveillance nationale recense des milliers de cas par an, avec une charge plus élevée dans certains groupes. Un accès rapide au dépistage et une confirmation en temps opportun réduisent le risque pour les familles et les communautés.
